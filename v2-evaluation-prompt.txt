You are the lead evaluator for the Genesis Creativity Engine. Your job is to assess the strategic quality of AI-generated games.

The V2 game database is at /Users/jamesbrowne/aigame/genesis_v2_run7.db and the codebase is at /Users/jamesbrowne/aigame/. Use python3 inspect_game.py --db-path genesis_v2_run7.db --game-id <game_id> --show-rules to load any game's rules.

For each of the top 10 games by Go Essence score, run this evaluation process:

Phase 1 — Rule Comprehension
Read the game's rules and topology. Produce a plain-English summary: board structure, how players take turns, what each action does, how captures/effects propagate, how you win. Flag any rules that are degenerate (e.g. "player 1 always wins by playing action 0 repeatedly").

Phase 2 — Strategic Play
Spin up two teammate agents as Player 1 and Player 2. They should:
- Play 3 full games against each other, reasoning through each move out loud
- After each game, reflect: What strategy did I use? What would I do differently? Did my opponent surprise me?
- After all 3 games, each player writes a short "strategy guide" for their side

Phase 3 — Strategic Analysis
Both players discuss together:
- Are there distinct viable strategies, or does one approach dominate?
- Is there meaningful counter-play (can you adapt to your opponent's strategy)?
- Is there short-term vs long-term tension (sacrifice now for advantage later)?
- Do emergent concepts arise (territory, influence, tempo, initiative)?
- Does the topology matter (do spatial relationships create strategic depth)?
- How does this game compare to known games (chess, Go, Hex, etc.) in feel?

Phase 4 — Verdict
For each game, produce a structured assessment:

Game ID: <id>
Rules Summary: <1-2 sentences>
Topology: <board structure>

SCORES (1-10):
  Strategic Depth: <score> — <why>
  Emergent Complexity: <score> — <why>
  Balance: <score> — <why>
  Novelty: <score> — <why>
  Replayability: <score> — <why>
  Overall "Would I play this again?": <score>

KILLER FLAWS: <any degenerate mechanics, dominant strategies, or broken rules>
BEST QUALITY: <what makes this game most interesting>
IMPROVEMENT IDEAS: <one rule change that would make it deeper>

Phase 5 — Rankings
After evaluating all 10 games, produce a final ranking with commentary. Identify which games, if any, have genuine strategic merit — and what structural properties the best games share that the worst ones lack. Feed these insights back as recommendations for the next evolution run.
