## Phase 1 — Rule Comprehension

**Board.** 9×9×9 cube, level-2 Menger sponge. 400 active of 729. Same substrate as `a6385db22c0b` and `b160b1f55378`.

**Turn structure.** Alternating, 1 piece/turn. P1 first. Max_turns = 100.

**Action space.** 730 actions = 729 placement + 1 pass. Place-only.

**Placement & capture.** Capture = **outnumber-3** (vs **outnumber-2** in `a6385db22c0b` siblings). When a stone is placed at `c`, every adjacent enemy stone `d` is checked; if `d` has **≥3** placer-coloured stones among its active neighbours (counting `c`), `d` is cleared. Verified empirically via `1,0,9,80,81`: P2's `(0,0,0)` is cleared once all 3 of its active neighbours `(1,0,0), (0,1,0), (0,0,1)` are P1.

**Propagation.** influence (radius=1, strength=1.0, decay=0.5). Captures do not reset board_values.

**Win condition.** Threshold-race > **57.974**. `target_dimension_p2 = -1`. Equal margins → draw. Max-turn timeout: highest sum.

**Pie rule.** Off.

**Degeneracy check.**
- The outnumber-3 capture has a strikingly different equilibrium from outnumber-2: in outnumber-2, P2 single-stone invasions in degree-2 cells get captured by 2 P1 neighbours (cost ~0.5 to P1). In outnumber-3, **degree-2 cells are *immortal* to capture** (only 2 active neighbours, can never reach 3 friendly). This means P2 has a viable invasion strategy in degree-2 cells that *cannot be cleared* — a structural difference from siblings.
- Conversely, **degree-3 cells with 3 surrounding enemy stones DO clear** (verified Game 3 below: P1's `(0,0,0), (0,2,0), (2,0,0), (2,2,0), (1,3,0)` all cleared in turn). The capture rule is *more* potent on these cells than I expected from the briefing's "captures fire less often" framing.
- Net: the outnumber-3 rule changes which cells are safe to invade and which are capture-traps. The decision tree per placement is genuinely different.

### Strategy guides

**P1 (offence/threshold push):** Open in a corner subcube with a *defensive* first stone — a corner cell `(0,0,0)` is degree-3 and gets wrapped fast. **Open at `(2,2,2)` instead** (degree-6, requires 3 P2 stones at specific neighbours to wrap; P2 must commit ~5 plies to set up). Keep the cluster *contiguous* so each new P1 stone has ≥1 P1 neighbour — the wrap requires 3 P2 neighbours, so a P1 with 1 friendly already has only 2 hostile neighbours possible.

**P2 (defence + threshold contest):** **Wrap-capture is genuinely viable.** Identify degree-3 P1 stones (corners, isolated edges) and play the 3 surrounding cells in succession. Each wrap costs P2 3 plies and gains a P1 stone clearance; P1's deposits at the cleared cell vanish. Net gain ~+1.5 effective per wrap. This is *better* than mirror-cluster against an aggressive P1.

## Phase 3 — Joint Strategic Analysis

**Distinct viable strategies?** **Two:**
1. **Corner cluster (P1)** — same as siblings. Captures don't fire.
2. **Wrap-capture (P2)** — exploits outnumber-3 by encircling degree-3 P1 stones. **Genuinely new strategy** not viable in outnumber-2 siblings.

**Counter-play.** Real and partial. P1 vs cluster-cluster: P1 wins by tempo (+6). P1 vs wrap-capture: P1 leads but by smaller margin and game runs longer. The wrap-capture strategy's existence pushes P1 to play *defensively* — favour high-degree internal cells, contiguous clusters — which itself is a meaningful strategic constraint.

**Short-term vs long-term.** **Tactical horizon = 3 plies** (the wrap pattern requires planning the third P2 placement ahead). **Strategic horizon = 5–8 plies** (P2 picks which P1 stone to encircle 3 plies in advance; P1 sees the wrap forming and chooses whether to defend or expand). This is meaningfully deeper than siblings.

**Emergent concepts observed.**
- **Wrap-capture pattern** — 3 P2 stones at the active neighbours of a degree-3 P1 stone clear it. New tactical primitive.
- **Degree-2 immortal invasion** — P2 stones in degree-2 cells cannot be captured, only outnumbered in score. Acts as influence-blocker (each degree-2 P2 stone reduces P1's neighbours by ~1.0 effective).
- **Cluster-cohesion as defence** — keeping P1 stones contiguous (each with ≥1 P1 neighbour) limits the wrap to ≤2 P2 friendlies on degree-3 cells, immunising them.

**Does menger matter?** **Yes, more than for siblings.** The fractal hole pattern creates the high count of degree-2 and degree-3 cells, which is precisely what the outnumber-3 rule responds to. **In a flat 9³ cube** (no holes), most cells would be degree 6, and the wrap would require 3-of-6 P2 friendlies — much harder to set up. The Menger structure *amplifies* the capture rule's strategic relevance.

**Does the propagation kernel matter?** Same as siblings — radius=1 gives cluster compounding. The capture-loss is ~1.5 effective per captured stone, which is ~2× the per-stone cluster gain. So losing one stone costs ~2 plies of build progress; this is enough to make capture economically meaningful.

**Capture-rule contribution.** **High.** Game 3 demonstrates 5 captures in 22 plies — very different from siblings where 0 captures fired across 50+ plies. Outnumber-3 generates a genuine capture economy on the menger.

**First-mover advantage / seat balance.** **Better than siblings.** Briefing's trained-vs-trained 0.333 (P2-favoured) is the inverse of `a6385db22c0b`'s 0.667 (P1-favoured). My Game 3 shows P2 has a viable wrap-strategy that doesn't exist for outnumber-2. Whether this fully balances is unclear from a 3-PPO-run sample, but the structural mechanism for P2 to keep up is real.

## Phase 4 — Novelty Adversary (mandatory)

**Adversary case.** This game is a re-skin of:

(a) **Threshold-race influence games** — same niche family as siblings.

(b) **Outnumber-3 capture** is **Tafl-family / Ataxx-family / Phutball** with a strict-encirclement variant. Specifically resembles **historical Tafl** where multiple-side encirclement clears a piece; 3-friendly is between standard Othello (2-bracket) and full surround (4+).

(c) **The combination "outnumber-3 + influence(r=1) + threshold-race"** is not directly in the published board-game corpus. It is partially anticipated by R10/R11 outnumber experiments. **Crucially, this game is structurally distinct from R19 menger top-1 `1f9191b5d4e6`** which used outnumber-2; the wrap-capture mechanic does not exist in R19's corpus.

(d) **Menger sponge substrate.** Same fractal substrate as siblings. **But the substrate's contribution is *positive* here**, not negative — the fractal hole pattern amplifies the wrap-capture rule by creating many degree-3 cells (the rule's natural target). This is an example where Menger geometry actively shapes equilibrium strategy.

(e) **Expert-transfer test.** A Tafl + Othello + Ataxx player would understand the rules in **3–5 minutes** — wrap-encirclement is unfamiliar to most modern players (Tafl is obscure), and the irreducible new piece is "outnumber-3 on a fractal degree distribution."

**Closest known-game analogue:** **Tafl-on-Menger with influence-sum scoring** — historical Tafl's encirclement-clear, on a fractal 3D substrate, with threshold-race scoring instead of king-escape.

**Comparison to R8's Connection Go (8/10 ceiling).** R8 had connection-win + custodian-flip on 2D. This game has threshold-race + wrap-capture on 3D Menger. **Different family.** R8's depth came from chain-completion via custodian flips. This game's depth comes from the wrap-capture forcing both sides to balance offence (build score) vs defence (avoid being wrapped). Thinner than R8 because no chain-completion, but **deeper than the outnumber-2 siblings** because captures fire in equilibrium.

**Comparison to R19 best.** R19 menger top-1 `1f9191b5d4e6` was outnumber-2 (4.8/10). **This R20 game is structurally distinct** — outnumber-3 enables the wrap-capture strategy. It is **deeper than R19 outnumber-2 menger games** by the size of the genuine P2 counter-strategy.

**Player rebuttal (P1 + P2).**
- The wrap-capture pattern under outnumber-3 is a *new* tactical primitive not in R19 or earlier outnumber siblings.
- Menger's degree distribution is *structurally relevant* to the rule (degree-3 cells = capture targets). Substrate matters here, not just as flavour.
- Subtractor: opening cluster-vs-cluster play (Game 1) is identical to siblings — the depth only emerges when one side commits to wrap-capture.
- Subtractor: the strategy diversity of 0.667 vs siblings' 1.000 reflects fewer viable strategies, but the existing strategies are deeper.

**Novelty score (post-adversary):** **5/10.** Above siblings (3/10) because:
- Wrap-capture under outnumber-3 is a *new tactical primitive* not present in R19 or R20 outnumber-2 corpus.
- Menger's degree distribution structurally amplifies the rule, not fragments it.
- Game 3 shows genuine P2 counter-strategy.

Below 7 because:
- Cluster-vs-cluster mainline is identical to siblings; depth only emerges under capture pressure.
- Outnumber-3 itself is a small parameter change from outnumber-2, not a fundamentally new mechanic.
- Tafl + Ataxx priors fully describe the rule space.

- **3-PPO-run sample size** is too small for the briefing's headline claims (P2-favoured 0.333). Need 15-seed re-run to confirm.

The **wrap-capture mechanic on degree-3 menger cells** is the game's crown jewel. A 3-stone P2 encirclement clears a P1 stone — a genuinely new primitive within Genesis that doesn't exist in outnumber-2 siblings or R19's family. Anchored against R19 menger top: this game is slightly more menger-coupled than R19's outnumber-2 mainline.
