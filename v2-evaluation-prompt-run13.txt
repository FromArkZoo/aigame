You are one evaluation team for the Genesis Creativity Engine Run 13. Your job is to assess the strategic quality of a single AI-generated game. Multiple teams will evaluate the same games independently; your output will be compared against theirs.

The Run 13 game database is at /Users/jamesbrowne/aigame/genesis_v2_run13.db and the codebase is at /Users/jamesbrowne/aigame/.

To load the game's rules, run:
    python3 inspect_game.py --db-path genesis_v2_run13.db --game-id <game_id> --show-rules

To play the game interactively between two agents:
    python3 play_helper.py --db-path genesis_v2_run13.db --game-id <game_id> [subcommand]

You will be told which specific game ID to evaluate. Do NOT evaluate other games.

=== TEAM COMPOSITION ===

Your team consists of three roles. Spin them up as separate teammate agents or handle them as distinct passes:

1. Player 1 — plays the game from the first-player perspective
2. Player 2 — plays the game from the second-player perspective
3. Novelty Adversary — actively argues the game is not novel; must be rebutted

Run the phases in order. Do not skip the Novelty Adversary phase.

=== PHASE 1 — RULE COMPREHENSION ===

Read the game's rules and topology. Produce a plain-English summary covering:
- Board structure (dimensions, axis size, topology type)
- Turn structure (who moves, how many pieces per turn)
- Action types (place, move, or both)
- Placement/movement constraints
- Capture or cellular-automaton dynamics (if any)
- Propagation or influence effects (if any)
- Win condition (with specific threshold values)

Flag any rules that are degenerate (e.g. "player 1 always wins by playing action 0 repeatedly", "game ends in ≤5 moves with a forced win", "capture rule is inert").

=== PHASE 2 — STRATEGIC PLAY ===

Play 3 full games between Player 1 and Player 2 agents. IMPORTANT constraints from pilot experience:

- **Every move MUST be engine-verified.** Run each move through `python3 play_helper.py --db-path genesis_v2_run13.db --game-id <game_id> --action play --moves "<comma-separated>"` before committing to it. Do NOT hand-reason about legality — the engine is authoritative. If a move you proposed is rejected, document that as a discovery about legal-action constraints.
- **Separate the reasoning.** When playing Player 1, do not read Player 2's reasoning until Player 1's move is committed, and vice versa. If you run Player 1 and Player 2 as the same agent sequentially, acknowledge the seat-swap bias in your Phase 3 analysis.
- **In game 3, swap seats.** Whoever was Player 1 in games 1-2 should play Player 2 in game 3. This reduces seat-identity bias.
- **Time budget per game.** Budget at most ~15 minutes of effort per game. If a game hits its max_turns cap (100 turns) and the position is still contested, STOP and note this as "non-convergent within max_turns — cannot be cleanly evaluated". Do not force-play to the cap.

For each move:
- Explain what you are considering
- State your chosen move and the reasoning (1-2 sentences)
- Predict the opponent's likely response

After each game, each player reflects:
- What strategy did I use?
- What would I do differently?
- Did my opponent surprise me?

After all 3 games, each player writes a short "strategy guide" for their side.

=== PHASE 3 — STRATEGIC ANALYSIS (joint) ===

Player 1 and Player 2 discuss:
- Are there distinct viable strategies, or does one approach dominate?
- Is there meaningful counter-play (can you adapt to your opponent's strategy)?
- Is there short-term vs long-term tension (sacrifice now for advantage later)?
- Do emergent concepts arise (territory, influence, tempo, initiative, ko fights)?
- Does the topology matter (do spatial relationships create strategic depth)?

=== PHASE 4 — NOVELTY ADVERSARY (MANDATORY) ===

The Novelty Adversary agent now takes the stage. Its role is to argue, as forcefully and specifically as possible, that this game is NOT novel. It must do at least the following:

(a) Compare the game against a broad catalog of known abstract strategy games. At minimum: Go, Hex, Y, Havannah, Reversi/Othello, Gomoku, Pente, Connect6, Amazons, Chameleon, Lines of Action, Mancala variants, Life-like cellular automata (Conway's Life, Day & Night, HighLife), Nim-like games. Cite specific rule correspondences.

(b) For CA-based games specifically: check against Life-like CA literature. Is this transition table a known CA rule, or a trivial perturbation of one? Is the "place + CA step" loop equivalent to any known CA-based game (e.g. Go-like games with background automata)?

(c) Argue the game is a re-skin of an existing game under a topology/coordinate transformation. Propose the specific transformation.

(d) Test the hypothesis "this is just <known game> in disguise" by asking: would an expert at that known game have an immediate advantage here?

Player 1 and Player 2 then rebut the Novelty Adversary. Their rebuttal MUST be specific — pointing to concrete strategic moments from Phase 2 playthroughs where a known-game analogy would have failed.

Resolution: the team jointly awards a Novelty score (1-10) reflecting how well the game survives adversarial novelty analysis. A game that is "X on a hex board" scores 2-3. A game with emergent dynamics that have no direct analog scores 7+. 10 means "this is genuinely a new abstract game worth documenting".
